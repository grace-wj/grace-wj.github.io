iii. PROJECT · A LOOM

WEFT

one thread per commit, one scarf per orbital year.

A physical jacquard loom, a Raspberry Pi, and a git hook. Once a day, the loom takes the day's commits and pulls them through the cloth.

The week the parser of slowcompiler was rewritten left three days of red thread; you can see it on the cloth — the red weft in an otherwise green spring.

v. ESSAY · WRITTEN AT LENGTH

ON STILLNESS

finished is the wrong unit.

An essay on the question of when a thing is finished. The argument is that finished is the wrong unit. Reads like a long letter; has been written for two years and may continue to be written for two more.

What I cross out is not gone — it is the shape this draft is not. Strikethroughs are kept. Some weeks have no notes. The cloth is still cloth.

“A gardener would recognize this. So would a conservator. Some things are only themselves while they're decaying.”
