PROOF · ISSUE XII · SPRING · MMXXVI

“what would happen if the artifact were kind?”

A brief life in software, ink, and language. Set in cyan, pink, and warm black. Pulled in your browser, while you watch.

Grace Wei-li Juan is a software engineer, polymath, and visual artist who lives at the intersection of several ways of making. She writes code that wants to be read like a poem, and essays that want to be compiled.

Her work asks a small, stubborn question: what would happen if the artifact were kind? The answer has looked, at various times, like a synthesizer, a loom, an essay about doing less, and an oil painting of a compiler in the act of parsing grief.

She is currently interested in the space between the finished thing and the continuing thing — programs that never quite terminate, scarves woven over a decade, paintings left out in the weather.
