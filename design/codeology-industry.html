Codeology × Industry

Event promotion for industry partnership talks — one design system per partner, three formats per event.

As Media Director at Codeology, I designed event promotion for partnership events with industry sponsors. The challenge: every partner had its own visual identity, and each design had to read as native to the partner’s brand while keeping Codeology’s presence intact.

Each event lived across three formats — a print flyer for tabling, square posts for the Instagram feed, and a 9:16 story tile. I built each one as a system from the start: typesetting at the widest format first, then reflowing the content to fit the constraints of each smaller crop.

For Uber, I leaned into their late-2010s gradient-ribbon system — tight uppercase headlines, generous black, colored arcs sweeping off-canvas. Codeology’s wordmark sits at small scale so the partner brand carries the visual weight.

Veeva’s identity was the opposite end: monochrome orange, 3D-rendered geometric props, thin sans-serif body. Here the partner’s visual language fills the canvas; Codeology shows up as a lockup mark and a typographic “Codeology ×” framing the wordmark.

Working inside external brand systems was a useful exercise in restraint — letting the partner’s visual identity drive the mood, while keeping the underlying information architecture (title, body, location, date, RSVP) consistent across every event in the series.
