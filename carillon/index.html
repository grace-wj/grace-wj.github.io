a small carillon

six bells, cast for six works. strike one. if it pleases you, strike a chord.

the bells are silent until you ring them.

headphones recommended. each bell decays for ten seconds; rings overlap into chords.

  1. a pocket synthesizer for people who cannot read music. you hum into it; it gives back a small song it thinks you meant. in quiet beta, ~120 weekly users.

  2. a compiler that takes thirty seconds to compile hello world and prints a haiku at each stage of the pipeline. five passes, five haiku.

  3. a physical jacquard loom, a raspberry pi, and a git hook. it weaves one scarf each year — one thread per commit. three threads in march were red.

  4. a series of three small oil paintings, shown one october in a borrowed window on bergen street. since dispersed to friends’ living rooms.

  5. an essay, gathered with two others under notebooks. it argues that a finished thing is the wrong unit. 2,400 words. last revised in november.

  6. ongoing, by post and by mail. write at any pace; replies will be slow and, when they come, longer than they should be. grayswan795@gmail.com
