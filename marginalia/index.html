Grace &
Wei-li Juan

being a brief life in software, ink, and language, with copious marginalia

Grace Wei-li Juan is a software engineer1, polymath2, and visual artist3 who lives at the intersection of several ways of making. She writes code that wants to be read like a poem, and essays that want to be compiled.

¶

Her work asks a small, stubborn question: what would happen if the artifact were kind? The answer has looked, at various times, like a synthesizer4, a loom5, an essay about doing less, and an oil painting of a compiler in the act of parsing grief.

She is currently interested in the space between the finished thing and the continuing thing — programs that never quite terminate, scarves woven over a decade, paintings left out in the weather.

¶
She suspects this is the only interesting territory left.

Works

hum§ is a pocket synthesizer for people who cannot read music. You hum into it; it gives you back a small song it thinks you meant. Slowcompiler§ is a compiler that takes exactly thirty seconds to compile "hello world" and prints a haiku at each stage. Weft§ is a physical loom, driven by git, that finishes one scarf a year.

Writing

Occasional essays, gathered under notebooks: On Stillness§, Notes Toward a Theory of Kitsch§, A Year Without Frameworks§.

Paintings

Small works in oil, ink, and gouache. A recent series, three bodies orbiting a lamp, was shown in a borrowed window on Bergen Street.

Elsewhere

Correspondence welcome: mail, github. Replies will be slow and, when they come, longer than they should be.

Colophon

Set in Fraunces and EB Garamond. Rubrication in vermilion. Typeset in the browser, one evening. Any errors are the compositor's.

The body is the main text. These are the glosses — read them or don't. A marginal reader is a generous one.

1 software engineer. Variously: compiler work, developer tools, small synthesizers. Prefers languages that leave fingerprints.

4 hum. Rust + WebAudio. ~4,200 LOC. In quiet beta; ~120 weekly users, most of whom cannot read music.

5 weft. A physical jacquard loom, a Raspberry Pi, and a git hook. One thread per commit; one scarf per orbital year.

Incipit liber Gratiae.

2 polymath. The word is immodest; she resists it. But she is curious in too many directions at once to fit inside engineer alone.

3 visual artist. Primarily oil; occasionally gouache. Occasionally ink, when patience is low.

A gardener would recognize this. So would a conservator. Some things are only themselves while they're decaying.
