Palm Trees (and Bougainvillea)

2026-05-14

I’m from Missouri. People in California know nothing about Missouri. They say, what’s the weather like there, and I take the opportunity to complain. It goes below 0° in the winter, above 100° in the summer, and it’s suffocatingly humid all year round—the charms of the Midwest. Then I’ll say something about how laughably gorgeous the weather here is, all the time. I try my best to make them feel guilty for living in such beautiful weather—the charms of a Midwesterner.

The change in temperature itself isn’t actually as important to me as the change in vegetation that comes with it. Palm trees don’t grow in Missouri, and I like palm trees. Before, I only ever saw them in movies depicting interesting and exciting things, or in the background of pictures depicting interesting and exciting things. Now I see them all the time, in person, and I can’t help looking. When I have the time, I’ll just stop walking and stare up at one for a while with a weird feeling of fascination. I think they’re beautiful, but the stronger pull is whatever subliminal association I have with them, that makes me think I’ve been transported into one of those old movies or photographs. It’s not exactly an original feeling: the small town girl moves to the big city, and is shocked at how tall the buildings are! But truthfully, it hits hard when you live with it.

Bougainvillea, too. Those hot pink flowers that show up everywhere on fences and sides of houses in the neighborhoods around here. When I first saw those, I thought, how the hell can flowers look that pretty and be real. Maybe I just really like pink. The one on the terrace of Davis Hall, between Cory and Kresge. I pass it every time I walk down to campus; it’s one of the small highlights of my morning.

There are times here when I feel overwhelmed. It’s in those times that these plants really become significant to me. I’m walking home at 10pm, coming from an exam I hated taking for a class I wish I didn’t have to. I’m stupid, I realize. That test made me realize I’m actually stupid. I have to apply to 300+ internships to get a shot at one, and I haven’t even started. People in my club have been doing this their whole lives; they have faang jobs and it’s not enough. What am I doing. I stop on the corner I always stop at, to look up at the line of palm trees I always look at. They’re so pretty against the night sky. Are they too tall for you? Is the sky here too big? Wanna go back? Back to that small, crushing place. There, I slept through the classes I didn’t skip, never got below an A, and I wanted to die. Here, I study notes until I dream about them, lay waste to my social life and physical health, and temporarily turn my life goal into absorbing every single existing discrete math tutorial on the internet in order to ultimately obtain a 55% on the cs70 midterm. Which do you prefer?
