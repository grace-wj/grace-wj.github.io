i. cadmium red study no. 1

cadmium red, deep

hum

a synth that listens · rust + webaudio · 2023—

You hum a melody into the microphone. hum writes down what it thinks you meant and plays it back, voiced for two oscillators and a reverb the size of a kitchen.

The hard part wasn’t the WebAudio graph. The hard part was deciding what a melody is when the singer is humming it badly. The pitch tracker is forgiving the way an aunt is forgiving — it rounds you toward the nearest tune that loves you.

About 120 weekly users. Most of them, at last poll, can’t read music. The compliment I keep, taped above the desk: a friend’s grandmother used hum to write a lullaby. The lullaby is two bars long and resolves on the wrong chord, which is why it works.

“the nearest tune that loves you” — from an evening note, march 14

rust · webaudio · ~4,200 LOC · two oscillators · one kitchen of reverb

ii. naples yellow study no. 2

naples yellow

slowcompiler

a compiler that takes thirty seconds · rust · in slow progress

It compiles a small functional language that resembles ML, if ML had been written by someone who’d just finished reading Bashō. At each stage of the pipeline it prints a haiku.

the door swings open
four small tokens take their seats
the room is now warm

~3,800 lines of Rust. The current stage I’m working on is the type checker, which I’m calling patience. I’m interested in tools that make you wait on purpose. The wait is a kind of editing — it gives the writer time to take it back.

read Bashō. then read him again. that’s the program.

rust · ~3,800 LOC · six pipeline stages · open source soon

iii. ultramarine study no. 3

ultramarine, french

weft

a loom driven by git · raspberry pi + jacquard kit

A small jacquard loom built from a kit, a Raspberry Pi, and a git hook. Each commit becomes a thread; each project a band of color. It finishes one scarf per orbital year.

Last year’s scarf is mostly grey, with three days of red — that was the week I rewrote slowcompiler’s parser. I wear it in November. It is, I’m told, slightly itchy.

the first computer was a loom (Jacquard, 1804). this is a half-joke about that.

jacquard kit · pi 4 · git hook · one scarf per orbital year

iv. burnt sienna study no. 4

burnt sienna

three bodies, orbiting

eight oils on linen, all 12×16 · 2022 — present

Three figures in domestic interiors, each lit by a single warm source. Eight panels, all on linen, all 12×16. The titles are mostly the names of friends.

Shown briefly in a borrowed window on Bergen Street; now in private collections, by which I mean my friends’ living rooms. The fourth painting in the series is in progress, very slowly.

the lamp is the same lamp in every painting. it lives on the desk.

oil on linen · 12 × 16 · sennelier when i can find it
