# concept: marginalia

A personal site in the shape of a **critical edition** of a living person. Running body text on a manuscript leaf, glosses and footnotes in both margins, works expand into footnoted modals-within-modals.

## Axis of weirdness
**Typographic hierarchy.** The information architecture is typographic, not navigational: title ▸ heading ▸ body ▸ marginal gloss ▸ footnote ▸ footnote-of-footnote. You read by descending the hierarchy, not by clicking through pages.

## What's in the leaf
- A prose bio as the main text
- Vermilion footnote markers that highlight their matching margin gloss on hover
- Pilcrow (¶) notes inset in the body that open margin glosses
- Each "work" link (hum, slowcompiler, weft, three essays) opens a modal styled as another manuscript leaf, with its own footnotes underneath

## Why it works for Grace
A polymath reading list is itself a critical edition of a life — primary text plus apparatus. This site admits that. A scholar's annotated copy of Grace.

## Structural oddness
- No nav. There is one page, and everything else is typography.
- Blackletter ("UnifrakturMaguntia") title over Fraunces/EB Garamond body — the typographic stack itself is the brand.
- Real drop cap (rubricated), italicized amp, centered section heads in rubric red
- Margin notes sit in a 3-column CSS grid at desktop; collapse to left-rule notes on mobile
- Modal pages have a second layer of footnotes under a ruled line
